3. Are DigiLocker and VeriFile same type of service?

No. In terms of architecture, security, and privacy, VeriFile is structurally different and far superior as compared to digital solutions such as DigiLocker.

Solutions like DigiLocker are built on a ‘centralized’ architecture for data storage and verification that stores user data on a server that is controlled by a third party. So, during the verification process, the user data is inherently exposed to the internet through various APIs. This leaves scope for malicious actors who can find a vulnerability in the security of the API and can access user databases, thereby, compromising the privacy and security of the user data.

Across the globe, numerous hacking incidents are vindicating the shortcomings of the centralized solutions and their vulnerability to malicious attacks. In fact, not only external hackers, even insiders with access to such centralized databases pose a threat to the integrity and privacy of the user databases.

On the contrary, VeriFile applies a ‘decentralized’ approach. By leveraging the open-source blockchain system of the popular cryptocurrency Ethereum as the underlying stack, VeriFile has created a proprietary method to compute and process unique fingerprints or hashes of documents generated by the issuers.

These hashes are irreversible in nature and are put on the blockchain against the Public Key assigned to the issuers. Any verifier can compare the hash generated for the VeriFile document with the data available on this blockchain, to verify the respective VeriFile document.

Since, these irreversible document hashes which are nothing but a string of random characters, are the only things exposed to the internet, the problem of data privacy is simply non-existent. Furthermore, these hashes are stored on the blockchain rather than a centralized database, making the entire verification system tamper-proof, instantaneously auditable, and free from any central point of failure.

4. How safe is my data with VeriFile?

Nobody has access to the user’s personal data, not even VeriFile.

While only issuers and document holders or beneficiaries gain access to the VeriFile document, VeriFile receives only irreversible hashes or unique fingerprints for documents from the issuers. These hashes are published on the blockchain for verification purposes.

The only data that a malicious actor, externally or even internally within VeriFile, can see are the document hashes, which are nothing but strings of gibberish.

5. How does a VeriFile document look like?

VeriFile files are pdf files that contains embedded blockchain proofs. They can be opened with any pdf viewer.

6. How do I share my VeriFile document with someone?

VeriFile file is just a simple pdf file. Just like any other document, folder or photo, you can attach it and share it via email, WhatsApp, NFC, Bluetooth, USB drive or any other means that you find convenient.

7. How do I verify a VeriFile document?

As a verifier, you will need to upload the pdf document on the verification portal mentioned on the document itself. This portal is maintained by the issuer of the same document. Alternatively, you can also verify the documents by using the portal on our website.

8. How much time does it take to Verify a document with VeriFile?

A few seconds only. Yes, verification of documents is extremely fast with VeriFile and you can do it from any corner of the world.

9. Do I need special software to verify a VeriFile document?

All you need is an internet connection and a smartphone or a computer. You can simply visit the verification portal mentioned on your document or visit the VeriFile website to validate your document.

10. Would I need to own a cryptocurrency wallet to store my Verifile documents?

Absolutely not. You neither need a cryptocurrency or blockchain wallet nor need any app to store your VeriFile documents. You can store the VeriFile PDF file on your usual storage means such as your computer, smartphone, USB drive, Google Drive, or any other cloud storage methods.

11. What happens if someone modifies the VeriFile document?

Any changes made to an issued VeriFile document will drastically change the document hash, making it invalid during the verification.
